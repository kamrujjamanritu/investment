Short Summary

We effortlessly manage remote medical devices, providing quick access for service to maintain patient care. This saves the companies a fortune in unnecessary travel ($200 to $1200 per trip), increases uptimes and reduces their carbon footprint.

Highlights
  • Founded in June 2020.
  • Security tested in 11 medical imaging facilities.
  • First beta customer Nov 2020.
The Business

Our SaaS software product, maiLink SRM, is used by medical device manufacturers and independent service companies to reduce unnecessary field service calls. It does this by providing comprehensive asset management for fleets of deployed products and through secure communications including telemetry, remote access, and command and control.

maiLink SRM helps both types of organizations save money, save time, deliver better customer service and reduce their carbon footprint. Plus, we have a disruptive business model that will change the way customers think about buying our type of services.

The Market

Our first vertical is in medical devices. There are about 16,000 target customers in this space globally, presenting a $1B ARR opportunity for us.

Our product is applicable across many industries ... medical devices are low-hanging fruit for us because we've been creating, building, and servicing medical devices for decades.

Team

Our team is well known in the medical devices space. Julian and Adam Zenner have worked together in three companies over the last 20 years, and know thousands of people in the industry. That makes recruiting easy because we have a loyal following of people willing to join, especially developers and sales and marketing folks.

Julian Marshall (CEO) went from the first employee (SW/HW Engineer) to VP Marketing at R2, which was sold to Hologic in 2006 ($220M). At Hologic, he ran global product management for the breast imaging product portfolio, growing revenue to $800M/annum and 80% market share (digital breast tomosynthesis).
